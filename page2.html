What do I need to have a healthy pregnancy?

Proper prenatal care.

Prenatal care is the healthcare you receive while pregnant. Regular prenatal care throughout your pregnancy helps to catch potential concerns early and reduces the risk of pregnancy and birth complications.

  • Seek prenatal care.

    During pregnancy, regular prenatal visits can help your health care provider monitor your health and the baby's health. Teens might need specialized prenatal care.

  • Eat a healthy diet.

    During pregnancy, a teen will need more folic acid, calcium, iron and other essential nutrients. A daily prenatal vitamin can help fill any gaps. You can find these vitamins in practically any pharmacy. Make sure to discuss with a health care professional about the best vitamins for you and your baby before buying any.

  • Stay physically active.

    Regular physical activity can help ease discomfort and boost your energy level. Encourage yourself to get a health care provider's OK before starting or continuing an exercise program, especially if you have an underlying medical condition.

  • Gain weight wisely.

    Gaining the right amount of weight can support the baby's health — and make it easier for you to lose weight after delivery.

  • Avoid risky substances.

    Alcohol, tobacco and any illegal drugs are off-limits during pregnancy. Even use of supplements and prescription and over-the-counter medications deserve caution.

  • Take childbirth classes.

    These classes can help prepare yourself for pregnancy, childbirth, breast-feeding and being a parent. Of course, you may not always be able to afford these classes, which is why there are websites where you can find resources exactly for this, like Pampers Website and youtube channels like Bridget Teyler's, a childbirth educator and doula whose youtube channel is packed with helpful advice and tips!

Healthy dieting!

Pregnancy is a great time to try and look after yourself and get lots of nutrients for you and your baby, giving him/her the best start in life. Try to eat lots of different foods every day and include something from each of the following main food groups;

Foods.

  • Bread, rice, potatoes or pasta (wholegrain if possible)

    Try to base your meals on these; they give you the energy you and your baby need.

  • Fruit and vegetables (fresh, frozen, dried or canned)

    Try to eat 5 different fruits and vegetables a day, a small glass of fruit juice can count as one. They contain lots of vitamins and minerals for your baby’s growth and development.

  • Meat, fish, eggs and beans

    Try to eat a couple of these a day; they are important for your baby’s growth. Canned fish is cheap and nutritious and so are canned peas, beans and lentils like baked beans, chick peas and red kidney beans.

  • Milk and dairy foods (cheese, yogurt) or dairy-free alternatives if you are vegan

    Try to eat a couple of these a day; they are important for your baby’s bones.

Drinks.

  • Water

    Water is the healthiest and cheapest!

  • Unsweetened fruit juice

    It can be a good source of vitamin C, but high in sugar, so stick to one small glass a day with your breakfast, lunch or dinner.

  • Semi-skimmed milk

    It can be enjoyed with a snack or on its own. It’s high in calcium so it‘s good for you and your baby's bones.

Try not to drink too many sugary drinks, like sugary fizzy drinks, and don’t forget – energy drinks, tea and coffee contain caffeine which you shouldn’t have too much of when you are pregnant.
